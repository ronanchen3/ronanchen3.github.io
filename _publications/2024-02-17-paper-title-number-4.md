excerpt:'Xin Yuandi, a literary supplement of the Nanjing Xinmin Daily published from 1935 to 1937, served as a crucial cultural front established by core Left-wing forces under the leadership of the Chinese Communist Party within the Kuomintang-controlled heartland. This publication strategically employed political essays, literary works, and artistic criticism as tools to implement the Left-wing wartime mobilization strategies. Its political essays, notably through symbolic campaigns like the “Da Gou movement (Anti-Traitor movement)”, shaped public opinion. Literary creations, including drama, poetry, and fiction, fostered national identity by utilizing trauma narratives and heroic portrayals. Concurrently, its literary criticism established a “united front” within the arts community by promoting theatrical reform and the National Defense Cinema movement. The historical significance of Xin Yuandi, however, extends far beyond its conventional propaganda function as a mouthpiece for the War of Resistance. More importantly, it offers a unique case study showcasing how, during a specific historical period and under immense political pressure, Left-wing forces successfully captured cultural hegemony, promoted the popularization of arts, and facilitated a pivotal transition from “proletarian literature” to “resistance literature”.'
